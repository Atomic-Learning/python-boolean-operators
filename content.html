Boolean Operators in Python

Python provides three boolean operators: not, and, and or. These allow you to combine boolean values and expressions to create more complex conditions.

The not Operator

The not operator reverses a boolean value:

print(not True) print(not False) print(not (5 > 3))

The and Operator

The and operator returns True only if both operands are True:

print(True and True) print(True and False) print(False and True) print(False and False)

It also works with comparison expressions:

print(5 > 3 and 2 < 4) print(5 > 3 and 2 > 4)

The or Operator

The or operator returns True if at least one operand is True:

print(True or True) print(True or False) print(False or True) print(False or False)

With comparison expressions:

print(5 > 10 or 2 < 4) print(5 > 10 or 2 > 4)
